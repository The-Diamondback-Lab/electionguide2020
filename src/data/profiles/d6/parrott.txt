<p class="c13"><span>A lifelong Marylander, Neil Parrott, a Republican, lives, works and raises his family in
    Maryland&#x2019;s 6th Congressional District, according to his </span><span class="c4"><a class="c2" href="https://www.parrott2020.org/front-page">campaign
      website</a></span><span class="c1">.</p>
<p class="c13"><span>Parrott is challenging Trone to represent the 6th Congressional District in the U.S. House of
    Representatives. He told</span><span class="c4"><a class="c2" href="https://bethesdamagazine.com/2020-general-election-voters-guide/u-s-congress-district-6/">&#xA0;Bethesda
      Magazine</a></span><span class="c1">&#xA0;he is running to give everyone a future with equal opportunity to
    succeed and to help families by keeping taxes low.</span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">He currently represents District 2A in the state House of Delegates, a position he
    has held since 2011. He has sponsored more than 60 bills, including several related to tax exemptions.
    &#xA0;</span></p>
<p class="c5"><span class="c1">Here is where he stands on the issues: </span></p>
<ul class="c17 lst-kix_3k9ejz8obstf-0 start">
  <li class="c5 c16"><span>Parrott promises to create more jobs by lowering taxes and redirecting manufacturing jobs
      to the United States. The U.S. also needs to implement business-friendly legislation and eliminate
      &#x201C;costly and unnecessary&#x201D; regulations, he told the </span><span class="c4"><a class="c2" href="https://www.heraldmailmedia.com/news/election_2020/maryland/trone-faces-two-challengers-for-congressional-sixth-district/article_172b6797-a7d8-5cc7-96e2-fa5e1ff16bbf.html">Herald-Mail</a></span><span class="c1">.</span></li>
  <li class="c0"><span>Parrott does not support the Green New Deal, </span><span>nor does he support environmental
      laws that increase costs of production and result in sacrificing manufacturing jobs, </span><span>he told
    </span><span class="c4"><a class="c2" href="https://www.baltimoresun.com/politics/elections/voter-guide/bs-voter-guide-parrott-congress-d6-20200518-sb2f43ngyncgjphc55ca3gprdu-story.html">The
        Baltimore Sun</a></span><span class="c1">. He also believes trade laws should be used to get other countries
      to pollute less.</span></li>
  <li class="c0"><span>Although he told </span><span><a class="c2" href="https://bethesdamagazine.com/2020-general-election-voters-guide/u-s-congress-district-6/">Bethesda
        Magazine</a></span><span>&#xA0;congressional stimulus packages helped families impacted by the COVID-19
      pandemic, Parrott said he does not support unemployment packages. He also believes schools should reopen and
      people should get back to work, with reasonable safety precautions, he told </span><span><a class="c2" href="https://www.heraldmailmedia.com/news/election_2020/maryland/trone-faces-two-challengers-for-congressional-sixth-district/article_172b6797-a7d8-5cc7-96e2-fa5e1ff16bbf.html">the
        Herald-Mail</a></span><span>.</span></li>
  <li class="c0"><span>Parrott does not think it is the Congress&#x2019; role to address racial disparities, he told
    </span><span class="c4"><a class="c2" href="https://www.heraldmailmedia.com/news/election_2020/maryland/trone-faces-two-challengers-for-congressional-sixth-district/article_172b6797-a7d8-5cc7-96e2-fa5e1ff16bbf.html">the
        Herald-Mail</a></span><span>. It is not the government&#x2019;s job to ensure equal outcomes for people, it
      is the government&#x2019;s job to provide equal opportunity, he said. He says quotas and
      &#x201C;institutionalized preferential treatment&#x201D; should be eliminated.</span></li>
</ul>
