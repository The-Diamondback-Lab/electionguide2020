<p class="c9"><span>Charles Anthony decided to run for office after watching the 2018 and 2020 State of the Union
    addresses, in which he thinks the Democrats displayed &#x201C;utter disrespect,&#x201D; he told </span><span class="c4"><a class="c2" href="https://bethesdamagazine.com/2020-general-election-voters-guide/u-s-congress-district-3/">Bethesda
      Magazine</a></span><span class="c1">.</span></p>
<p class="c9"><span class="c1">Anthony first ran for the 3rd Congressional District seat in 2018. He lost to John
    Sarbanes, who has served as the representative for the district since 2007. Anthony had 28.3 percent of the
    votes. </span></p>
<p class="c9"><span>In this election, in which he is challenging Sarbanes again, Anthony has continued to express
    dissatisfaction with the Democratic Party. If elected, he told</span><span><a class="c2" href="https://bethesdamagazine.com/2020-general-election-voters-guide/u-s-congress-district-3/">&#xA0;</a></span><span class="c4"><a class="c2" href="https://bethesdamagazine.com/2020-general-election-voters-guide/u-s-congress-district-3/">Bethesda
      Magazine</a></span><span class="c1">, he would support decreasing the amount of government interference in the
    economy and the health care system.</span></p>
<p class="c9"><span>Anthony is a retired U.S. Army officer, where he was a lieutenant colonel and worked in hospital
    administration and medical service corps, and a retired federal employee from Walter Reed Army Medical
    Center.</span></p>
